A Streamlit tool that queries the PubChem and ClinicalTrials.gov APIs to aggregate bioactivity data for a given gene target. Ranks potential chemical binders by dose-response and binding affinity, and displays an interactive activity landscape with searchable compound details including synonyms, assay data, and clinical trial linkages.

The challenge

When trying to identify potential drug candidates for a given gene target, relevant data is scattered across multiple public databases — bioactivity measurements in PubChem, compound metadata in ChEMBL, and clinical context in ClinicalTrials.gov. On top of that, activity values across assays come in inconsistent units and aren't always directly comparable. There's no easy way for a researcher to quickly get a structured, ranked view of what compounds are worth investigating.

I wanted to explore whether I could use AI and public APIs to automate that aggregation into something genuinely useful — a tool that does the tedious data wrangling and surfaces the signal.

What was important to get right

  • Normalizing activity units — all EC50, IC50, Ki, and Kd values are converted to nM so compounds are actually comparable
  • Separating dose-response from binding affinity — these measure different things and shouldn't be ranked together; the tool surfaces top 5 for each independently
  • Clinical trial linkage — each compound is matched to ClinicalTrials.gov records using name and synonym search, giving real-world context on how far along a compound is
  • Interactive activity landscape — a scatter plot of all compounds by activity value and type, color-coded by clinical trial status, so you can quickly spot promising candidates at a glance
  • Searchable compound details — any compound in the dataset can be looked up by CID to see its full metadata, assay history, and trial info
