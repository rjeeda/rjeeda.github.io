Drivers of Ocean Microbiome Diversity

Python scikit-learn scikit-bio Random Forest PCoA

Used PCoA and Random Forest modeling on the Tara Oceans dataset — 139 globally sampled ocean microbiomes — to identify depth and temperature as the strongest drivers of community composition. Compared taxonomic vs. functional abundance as environmental predictors and surfaced biological markers for specific oceanic conditions.

GitHub →

Background

Earth's oceans are a vast reservoir of microbial diversity, and oceanic microbes play crucial roles in global carbon and nutrient cycling. Yet the environmental factors driving changes in these communities are not well understood — particularly in deeper ocean layers, where data is sparse compared to surface ecosystems.

This project analyzed the Tara Oceans dataset, the most comprehensive global survey of ocean microbiome composition to date. For each of 139 samples, both taxonomic (16S rRNA OTU) and functional (eggNOG gene family) abundance data were available alongside environmental metadata including depth, temperature, salinity, oxygen, and nutrient concentrations.

Key questions

  • Do the same environmental factors drive variation in both taxonomic and functional composition?
  • How does the relationship between taxonomic and functional richness change across environmental gradients?
  • Which specific taxa or functional genes are most associated with particular oceanic conditions?

Approach & findings

PCoA on taxonomic abundances revealed that depth and temperature were the dominant drivers of community structure, while PCoA on functional abundances pointed instead to longitude and latitude — suggesting geographic specialization shapes function differently than taxonomy.

Random Forest models confirmed depth and temperature as the most predictable variables from both data types, with taxonomic inputs outperforming functional inputs overall. Richness analysis showed that while taxonomic richness varies considerably across depth and temperature gradients, functional richness stays comparatively stable — implying that key functions are maintained even as community membership shifts.

Feature importance analysis surfaced specific biological markers: the SAR324 clade was present almost exclusively in anaerobic conditions, two transcriptional regulators appeared only in deep oxygen minimum zones, and Acidobacteria were enriched at high nitrate concentrations — prompting new hypotheses about their role in marine nitrogen cycling.
